## benchmark-reporter — multi-thread the callback producer + locate the ceiling

Continuing the ElasticQueue field-test prep ([[thread-elastic-queue-bdb-to-file]], PR #137). Eric asked to
multi-thread the callback producer to mimic real-world concurrent clients (the workers=1-vs-50 comparison in
the prior log showed callback throughput plateauing at ~74k ops/s and I flagged it as producer- and/or
dispatch-bound).

**Added `bench.callback.producers` (default 1)** (`13d6da44`): N producer threads share one global in-flight
semaphore, firing `asyncRequest` concurrently. Separates producer-bound from dispatch-bound.

**Finding — the single-route ceiling is the per-route dispatch VT, not the producer.** At workers=50, 200k
ops, in-flight 2000:
- producers=1 → 76,960 ops/s
- producers=8 → 83,438 ops/s
- producers=16 → 84,182 ops/s (plateau)

Multi-threading the producer added only ~9% then flattened, so the ~84k ops/s ceiling for **one route** is the
**per-route dispatch virtual thread** (ElasticQueue is ordered-FIFO-per-route: a single VT drains it and hands
off to the worker pool), not the client and not the workers. Latency ~24 ms tracks Little's law
(2000 / 84k ≈ 23.8 ms). **Implication:** scaling a route past its dispatch ceiling is horizontal (more
routes/instances), not more producers or workers — consistent with how Mercury scales. Default kept at 1
(producers barely affects the dispatch-bound result, so comparability is preserved).
